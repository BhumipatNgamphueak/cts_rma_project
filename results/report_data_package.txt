  CTS INT l=16: +1102.3 → +52.2% vs FULL
  CTS EXT l=16: +756.9  → +4.5% vs FULL

For CTS at l=32 (relative to CTS FULL l=32 = 765.4):
  CTS INT l=32: +1079.7 → +41.1% vs FULL
  CTS EXT l=32: +750.7  → −1.9% vs FULL (EXT ≈ FULL, negligible diff)

For RMA2 at l=16 (relative to RMA2 FULL l=16 = 1035.3):
  RMA2 INT l=16: +1071.1 → +3.5% vs FULL
  RMA2 EXT l=16: +1021.9 → −1.3% vs FULL (all within noise)

Conclusion:
  CTS shows strong sensitivity to priv_mode at intermediate l:
    Internal only > Full > External only  (INT > FULL > EXT for CTS)
  RMA2 shows negligible sensitivity to priv_mode at l=16:
    All three within 5% of each other
  This difference arises from the distillation mechanism:
    CTS student must reconstruct zt from obs history;
    noisy external signals make reconstruction harder → INT easier to distil.
    RMA2 Phase-1 encoder learns from oracle xt directly → any subset works equally.

SECTION 8 — IMPLEMENTATION vs PAPER DISCREPANCY NOTE
================================================================================

Paper (Zhuang et al., 2023) privileged state x_t ∈ R^24:
  External (10D): contact_force_xyz(3), contact_flag(1), joint_torques(3), joint_accels(3)
  Internal (14D): friction(1), restitution(1), mass_delta×4(4), payload(1),
                  Kp_delta×3(3), motor_strength_delta×3(3), delay_norm(1)

Our implementation x_t ∈ R^33:
  External (13D): cf_vec(3), cf_flag(1), joint_torques(3), joint_accels(3), push_force(3)
  Internal (20D): friction(1), restitution(1), mass_delta×4(4), payload(1),
                  Kp_delta×3(3), motor_str_delta×3(3), delay_norm(1), Kd_scale(3), CoM_offset(3)

Added vs paper: push_force(3), Kd_scale(3), CoM_offset(3) = +9 dims total
Rationale: Extended DR range requires richer privilege for teacher to condition on.
This should be noted in the report as "our implementation extends x_t to 33D
to cover the expanded DR parameter space."

Encoder architecture unchanged from paper concept; RMA Phase-1 actor sees o_t+z_t,
critic sees o_t+x_t (33D). CTS teacher encoder: 33D→l, student: 75D history→l.

SECTION 10 — QUICK SUMMARY FOR ABSTRACT / CONCLUSION
================================================================================

1. All three methods achieve 100% episode-length success across all DR scales (1.0–2.0×),
   indicating that DR-trained policies generalize well to mild and moderate OOD.

2. RMA2 FULL (l=16) achieves the best reward among standard configurations:
   +1035.3 ± 117.0 (ID), +1009.8 ± 99.5 (hard OOD), 2.5% degradation.

3. CTS outperforms RMA2 when privileged information is restricted to internal parameters:
   CTS INT l=32: +1079.7 ± 77.5 with only 2.6% OOD degradation.
   The concurrent distillation mechanism is more robust to partial privilege.

4. CTS FULL underperforms when latent dimension is mismatched (l=16,32,64):
   External contact signals are noisy and difficult for the student to reconstruct
   from proprioceptive history, depressing CTS FULL performance at moderate l.

5. Hypothesis confirmed (Experiment 3): CTS FULL with l=128 achieves +1091.8 ± 113.7,
   matching CTS INT performance. Large latent capacity allows the teacher encoder to
   disentangle stable internal from noisy external signals.

6. Baseline (DR-only, no teacher) achieves +815.7 ± 146.6 — surprisingly competitive.
   This suggests DR alone provides a strong foundation; teacher-student adds +30% peak
   over baseline only when priv_mode and latent dimension are well-matched.
